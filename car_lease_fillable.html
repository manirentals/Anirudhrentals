1. Definitions and Interpretation

1.1 Damage Liability Fee (DLF) specifies the maximum predetermined financial ceiling the Lessee is subject to in the event of loss or damage.

1.2 Fair Wear and Tear covers reasonable vehicle deterioration as established by standard automotive leasing guidelines.

1.3 PPSA signifies the Personal Property Securities Act 2009 (Cth).

2. Term, Usage and Asset Constraints

2.1 This agreement grants a bailment to operate the vehicle; it provides no transfer of equity or title to the Lessee.

2.2 Usage strictly prohibits racing, off-road driving, sub-leasing, hazardous transport, or operation under identical restrictive conditions (e.g., intoxication).

2.3 Rent accumulation beyond the end-date without explicit permission shall trigger immediate repossession clauses.

3. PPSA Security Implementation

3.1 The Lessee irrevocably permits the Lessor to register a PMSI (Purchase Money Security Interest) on the national PPSR regarding the vehicle.

4. Financial Obligations & Tolling

4.1 The Security Bond acts in trust and will be returned net of legitimate deductions (tolls, missing fuel, exterior damage) within 14 standard days post-return.

4.2 The Lessee assigns the Lessor complete authority to statutorily nominate the Lessee for any road infringements, camera offenses, and toll collections incurred during possession.

5. Damage Liability Voiding Triggers

5.1 The protective DLF ceiling becomes immediately void, assigning full cost repair to the Lessee, if damage results from:

  • Submersing the vehicle in terrain elements (floods, beaches);
  • Misfueling the vehicle architecture;
  • Operating under criminal intent or severe negligence resulting in theft (e.g. keys left unattended).

Execution confirms the parties have rigorously read, apprehended and accepted the terms, particularly those assigning financial bounds and security implementations.
